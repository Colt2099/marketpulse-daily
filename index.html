Analysis of the Day

Friday's tape was split down the middle. The Nasdaq slipped 0.24% for its fifth consecutive losing session, the S&P 500 eased 0.27% and the Dow gave back 0.09%, while the small-cap Russell 2000 inched up 0.07%. The drag came from semiconductors: Cisco fell 4.37%, AMD dropped 2.06% and NVIDIA lost 1.64% after a New York Times report that OpenAI is weighing a delay to its IPO, which soured sentiment across AI-linked names. Yet the megacap complex was anything but uniform — Microsoft jumped 5.71%, Palantir 5.28%, Adobe 4.82%, Netflix 4.10%, Apple 3.14% and Amazon 2.50% all rebounded from the prior day's selloff, even as Alphabet (-1.84%) and JPMorgan (-1.81%) lagged. Defensives kept their bid, with Johnson & Johnson up 3.99% and UnitedHealth 2.97%, and beaten-down small caps bounced hard — Lucid surged 15.63% and Rivian 5.18%.

The macro backdrop stayed cautious. The 10-year Treasury yield eased to 4.40% with the fed funds rate at 3.63%, while CPI is still running at 4.2% year over year — above the Fed's target — and unemployment holds at 4.3%. Gold climbed 1.20% to roughly $4,096 and silver added 0.72%, extending a safe-haven bid that has run alongside the equity wobble. CNN's Fear & Greed Index slid to 23 ("Extreme Fear") from 28 a day earlier — its lowest in days — as a fifth straight Nasdaq decline and the chip-led drift kept investors defensive even while individual megacaps rebounded.
