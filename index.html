Analysis of the Day

Thursday's session masked a sharp divide beneath a calm headline tape. The S&P 500 closed essentially unchanged (-0.01%) and the Nasdaq fell 0.46%, but the damage was concentrated in megacap technology. Apple slid 6.12% and Microsoft lost 3.46% after both signaled price increases on consumer hardware — from iPhones to Xbox — as a response to surging memory-chip costs, a reminder that the AI-driven memory squeeze is now reaching end products. Palantir dropped 5.49%, Amazon fell 3.10% and Meta gave back 2.65%. With the heaviest index weights under pressure, it took strength elsewhere to keep the broad market flat: the Dow added 0.14% and the small-cap Russell 2000 outperformed with a 0.71% gain, a textbook rotation out of expensive, crowded leadership into healthcare, financials and industrials. UnitedHealth (+2.40%), Johnson & Johnson (+1.61%), JPMorgan (+0.50%) and Exxon Mobil (+0.47%) led the defensive bid, while AMD bucked the chip selloff with a 2.47% advance. Notably, Micron's blowout earnings still could not lift the Nasdaq as traders trimmed big-tech exposure.

The macro backdrop reinforced the cautious tone. The 10-year Treasury yield held at 4.41% with the fed funds rate at 3.63%, while CPI is still running at 4.2% year over year — above the Fed's target — and unemployment sits at 4.3%. Gold rose 0.97% to roughly $4,048 and silver added 0.47%, a move consistent with safe-haven and inflation-hedge demand on a risk-off day. CNN's Fear & Greed Index at 28 ("Fear") rounds out the picture: with sentiment defensive and money rotating away from the megacaps that have driven the market, breadth — not the megacap complex — did the heavy lifting on Thursday.
